Introduction

Allu Arjun (born 8 April 1982) is an Indian actor who predominantly works in Telugu films. He is one of the highest paid actors in South India[3] and known for his dancing abilities,[4] Allu is a recipient of several awards, including five Filmfare Awards South and five Nandi Awards.[5] Since 2014, he has been featured in Forbes India's Celebrity 100 list based on his income and popularity.[6]
Allu made his debut with Gangotri in 2003. He rose to prominence starring in Sukumar's cult classic Arya (2004), for which he earned a Nandi Special Jury Award.[7] Allu consolidated his reputation with the action films Bunny (2005) and Desamuduru (2007). In 2008, Allu starred in the romantic drama Parugu, for which he won his first Filmfare Award for Best Actor – Telugu.[8]
Allu went on to star in notable films such as Arya 2 (2009), Vedam (2010), Julayi (2012), Race Gurram (2014), S/O Satyamurthy (2015), Rudhramadevi (2015), Sarrainodu (2016), DJ: Duvvada Jagannadham (2017), Ala Vaikunthapurramuloo (2020), and Pushpa: The Rise (2021). His performances as a lower class cable operator in Vedam and as a carefree street smart man in Race Gurram won him two more Filmfare Awards for Best Actor – Telugu. Allu also won the Filmfare Award for Best Supporting Actor for his portrayal of prince Gona Ganna Reddy in Rudhramadevi. Allu received huge acclaim for his performance in Pushpa: The Rise, which emerged as the highest-grossing Indian film in 2021, and ranks among the highest-grossing Telugu films of all time.

Early Life

Allu Arjun was born on 8 April 1982[a] in a Telugu family in Madras (present-day Chennai) to film producer Allu Aravind and Nirmala. His paternal grandfather was the film comedian Allu Ramalingaiah. Their native place is Palakollu of West Godavari district, Andhra Pradesh.[15] Allu grew up in Chennai before their family moved to Hyderabad in his 20s.[16][17]
He is the second of three children. His elder brother Venkatesh is a businessman while his younger brother Sirish is an actor. His paternal aunt is married to Chiranjeevi.[18] Allu's cousins, namely, Ram Charan, Varun Tej, Sai Dharam Tej, and Niharika Konidela are also actors.

Career beginnings

After playing as a child artist in Vijetha (1985) and as a dancer in Daddy (1986), Allu made his adult debut in Gangotri.[20] The film was directed by K. Raghavendra Rao with his father Allu Aravind producing, along with C. Ashwini Dutt.[21] Praising his acting performance, Jeevi of Idlebrain criticized his looks in the film and added that "Arjun should choose roles that amplify his strength and nullify his weaknesses."[22] Allu then appeared in Sukumar's Arya.[23] He plays "Arya", an outgoing and free-spirited boy falling in love with Geetha (Anu Mehta), an introverted girl who is on the shield of another person Ajay (Siva Balaji).[24] The film was his breakthrough, earning a first Filmfare Best Telugu Actor Award nomination and winning a Nandi Special Jury Award at the Nandi Awards of 2008 ceremony, two CineMAA Awards for Best Actor and Best Actor Jury.[25] The film was a critical and commercial success,[26] grossing over ₹30 crore,[27] with a production budget of ₹4 crore.[28] In 2006, the film was dubbed and released in Malayalam in Kerala. Owing to the film's success, Allu received wide acclaim across the region and the Malayali people.[29]
He next starred in V. V. Vinayak's Bunny playing "Bunny", a college student.[30] Being successful at the box office, critics praised his efforts, mannerisms and dancing.[31][32] His next film was A. Karunakaran's musical love story Happy.[33] A remake of 2004 Tamil film Azhagiya Theeye, the film was a box-office bomb.[34] A critic appreciated his dancing skills and performance, but felt that his character is a typical happy-go-lucky guy.[35]

Experimentation of generes

He then starred in Puri Jagannadh's action film Desamuduru in which he played the role of Bala Govindam, a fearless journalist who falls for a woman with a darker past.[36] The film was a commercial hit, fetching him a Santosham Film Award, a CineMAA Award and a nomination for the Filmfare Award for Best Actor – Telugu.[37] The same year, he made his second cameo appearance alongside Chiranjeevi, in the song "Jagadeka Veerudiki" from the film Shankar Dada Zindabad.
His next film was Bhaskar's Parugu, where he played the role of Krishna, a happy-go-lucky man from Hyderabad who helps his friend to elope with his love, only to experience the wrath of the woman's father and the emotional struggle he felt. Idlebrain wrote: "Allu Arjun is pretty excellent in the first half as the characterization in the first half is vibrant and needs loads of energy. He carried the entire first half on his shoulders. He excelled in the emotional scenes in the second half."[38] Writing for Rediff.com, Radhikha Rajamani stated that "Arjun performs well though he is rather subdued."[39] Allu won his first Filmfare Award for Best Actor – Telugu and second Nandi Special Jury Award.

Further Success

He next appeared in V. V. Vinayak's action film Badrinath (2011), in which he played the role of Badri, a modern-day Indian samurai who is assigned to protect the Badrinath Temple by his Guru (Prakash Raj), to whom he is very loyal.[54] Allu undertook intensive martial arts and sword fighting training in Vietnam[55][56] and marks his first pairing with Tamannaah.[57] He grew his hair out for his look as a warrior in the film.[citation needed] It his second A-rated film in India after Vedam (2010), on the account of graphic action violence.[58] The film completed a 50-day run in 187 movie theaters.[59] His performance and character received mixed reviews.[60][61] A critic of The Times of India wrote that "Arjun hardly has any scope to emote as he was mostly pushed into action scenes and song sequences."[62]
After Badrinath, Allu signed Trivikram Srinivas's action comedy film Julayi in 2011.[63] It was released in 2012, in which he played the role of Ravindra Narayan, a street-smart yet spoilt brat whose life takes a drastic turn after he becomes the witness of a huge bank robbery. A critic wrote that "Allu Arjun puts in a confident performance as the loveable rogue. It's a role that is right up his alley and he carries it off with a characteristic panache. He lights up the screen with his dancing in particular, pulling off some pretty challenging dance moves."[64] Riya Chakravarty of NDTV, in her review, stated that "Arjun has delivered an outstanding performance."[65] He was nominated for the SIIMA Award for Best Actor (Telugu), for his performance in the film.

Commercial Success

In 2014, he appeared in a cameo role in Vamsi Paidipally's action thriller film Yevadu, alongside Kajal Aggarwal.[69][70] Y. Sunita Chowdary of The Hindu, in her review, wrote that "Allu Arjun shows what an actor can do even in a short role, in the few minutes he packs his experience, internalises the character and makes an impressive exit even as he loses his identity."[71] His next film was Surender Reddy's Race Gurram, in which he played Allu Lakshman "Lucky" Prasad, a carefree guy.[72] He joined the production of the film in May 2013.
The film was successful at the box office, with the film being Allu's first ₹100 crore grosser.[74] Writing to Deccan Chronicle, Suresh Kavirayani felt that Allu Arjun steals the show with his energetic performance and also praised his dancing skills in the film.[75] Ranjani Rajendra too praised his dance moves, comic acting and performance in the action sequences, but felt that the story is predictable and routine.[76] He won his third Filmfare Award for Best Actor – Telugu[77] and was nominated for SIIMA Award for Best Actor (Telugu) for the second time after Julayi. Allu produced and acted in a short film I Am That Change, which was released in August 2014. The film was directed by Sukumar,[78] to spread awareness on individual social responsibility. Upon its release, the short film received viral response online and was acclaimed by many including celebrities for its concept and execution.[79] It was screened in movie theaters across Andhra Pradesh and Telangana.[80]

Alavaikuntapuramlo and beyond

In December 2018, his next project Ala Vaikunthapurramuloo was announced, with Trivikram Srinivas as the director.[106] Pooja Hegde was selected to play the female lead role,[107] thus marking his second collaboration with Hegde after DJ: Duvvada Jagannadham.[108] He joined the production of the film in April 2019.[109] Produced jointly by Allu Aravind and S. Radha Krishna, the film marks the third collaboration of Allu and Trivikram after Julayi (2012) and S/O Satyamurthy (2015).[110] The film was released in January 2020.[111] The plot of the film follows Bantu (Allu) who is hated and neglected by his father Valmiki (Murali Sharma). Bantu later learns that he was swapped as an infant and his biological father is an affluent businessman, Ramachandra (Jayaram).
Bantu enters Ramachandra's house, Vaikuntapuram to protect his biological family when it is threatened by an influential man (Samuthirakani). Allu's performance received highly positive reviews. A review posted by Rohit Mohan on Koimoi said that "Be it comedy, action, emotions, style and of course dance, the star actor nails it in the film, as ‘Bunny’ made to audience groove to his tunes with his act."[112] Film Companion felt that Allu's performance in the board-room scene was the highlight of the film.[113] Bhavana Sharma of International Business Times, opined that Allu went into the skin of the character.[114] The film his career's highest grosser and stands among the highest-grossing Telugu films. Allu's dance performance in the song "Butta Bomma" received wide response.

Personal life
